# Chapter 21 — Measuring What Matters

A design system can appear successful because its library is large, its documentation is polished, or its adoption dashboard shows many imports. Those signals are useful, but they do not prove that the organization is building better products.

AINDS measures both system adoption and organizational outcomes.

Useful signals include:

- Percentage of product UI using canonical components and tokens
- Reduction in duplicate components and variants
- Time from design intent to reviewed Storybook implementation
- Accessibility defects found before and after adoption
- Consistency of terminology and interaction patterns
- Contribution and review cycle time
- Migration progress and deprecation completion
- New-hire time to productive contribution
- Quality of AI-generated output against system contracts
- Frequency of human escalation and the reasons for it

AI quality requires its own evaluation. A generated screen should be checked for component selection, token use, pattern conformity, content language, accessibility, responsiveness, test coverage, and whether it invented unsupported concepts.

Metrics should help teams learn rather than punish local variation. A low adoption number may reveal missing system capability, poor documentation, incompatible release timing, or lack of trust—not resistance.

Qualitative evidence matters too. Interview designers, engineers, product managers, and accessibility specialists. Ask where the system removes decisions, where it creates friction, and where people still rely on private knowledge.

Product outcomes should be connected where evidence supports the relationship. Improved form patterns may reduce errors or abandonment. Better consistency may improve confidence. Faster delivery may shorten time to test a product hypothesis. The system should avoid claiming causation it cannot demonstrate.

A small scorecard reviewed regularly is more valuable than a large dashboard no one uses.

> **Measure whether the system helps the organization make better decisions faster—not merely whether people import its packages.**

## Key takeaways

- Adoption is necessary but not sufficient.
- Measure quality, coordination, accessibility, and AI conformity.
- Use metrics diagnostically rather than as compliance weapons.
- Connect system changes to product outcomes carefully and honestly.
