# Chapter 1 — Why AINDS?

A senior engineer leaves the company after fifteen years.

The component library remains.  
The Figma files remain.  
Storybook remains.  
The design tokens remain.

Yet within months, new products begin behaving differently.

Buttons validate differently.  
Dialogs behave differently.  
Error messages vary.  
Accessibility regresses.  
New engineers make reasonable decisions that do not match previous ones.

What disappeared was not the components.

It was the knowledge.

AINDS exists to prevent that.

## The Core Problem

Traditional design systems preserve reusable assets.

AINDS preserves reusable knowledge.

A component library can tell a team what button to use. It cannot always tell the team when a button should be disabled, when validation should occur, what error message should appear, where focus should move, or why one pattern is preferred over another.

Those decisions often live as tribal knowledge.

AINDS transforms that tribal knowledge into organizational knowledge.

## The Shift

Most AI tools ask:

> What should I generate?

AINDS asks:

> What should I know before I generate?

That is the foundation of AINDS.

## Principles

- Knowledge is the source of truth.
- Generation is a consumer of knowledge.
- Components are not enough.
- Decisions must be reusable.
- AI should not invent what the organization has failed to document.

## Implementation Guidance

Begin by identifying inconsistent product decisions, undocumented conventions, and patterns that depend on senior team memory.

Capture those as knowledge articles, ADRs, pattern documents, and component guidance.

## Knowledge Before Generation

Before AI generates a component, workflow, icon, or page, it must understand the relevant organizational knowledge that governs that artifact.
